IrishMAN is external discovery/research material only. Its removed natural-language provenance and
qualified rights language mean its top-level MIT label cannot license every underlying melody,
transcription, arrangement, or annotation. Public outputs are limited to revision, split/index,
hashes, non-reconstructable fingerprints, confidence, aggregate facts, and matching code. The
adapter requires an explicit external cache and fails clearly when files are absent or mismatched.
