# EXP-008A candidate-family matrix

Audit date: 2026-07-19

Phase A verified a possible historical-score and independent-transcription route. Phase B asks a
different question: can two historically documented families of exactly four settings each be
identified, page-located, rights-cleared, and transcribed without lowering the evidence standard?

## Evidence standard

Title similarity and algorithmic similarity are insufficient. Each admitted setting needs a printed
or scholarly cross-reference, exact edition/tune/page identity, tune-specific authorship review, and
a readable source image. No IrishMAN row or unlicensed ABC can supply missing provenance.

## Required per-setting matrix status

No setting is admitted. Consequently fixture IDs, precise alternative titles, printed pages, scan
pages, image references, composer status, family-membership evidence, and rights confidence are
intentionally not fabricated. The available evidence does not establish eight records meeting every
required field.

Human clarification would need to nominate two families and provide or approve authoritative
cross-references and exact score pages for four settings per family. Only then should a transcriber
perform page-by-page identity, attribution, and legibility review.

DEFER — SUITABLE TWO-FAMILY SOURCE SET NOT VERIFIED

## Revised 4×2 gate

The original two-family-by-four-settings gate was not met, and no metadata was invented or
provenance standard relaxed to manufacture a passing result. The revised design instead requires
four documented tune families with exactly two independently verified historical settings per
family. Every setting must still meet the same exact-source, tune-specific rights, readable-score,
and documentary family-evidence standard described above. The experimental fixture therefore
remains eight settings in total. This scientific scope correction was made before any fixture,
QAOA, or hardware experiment was created or run.
